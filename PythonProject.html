Exploratory Data Analysis of Fashion Retail Sales and Returns¶

Project Objective¶

Conducted end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a Fashion Boutique dataset using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) to derive actionable business insights. Performed data cleaning, preprocessing, and statistical analysis to evaluate brand performance, product distribution, pricing trends, and inventory patterns. Developed meaningful visualizations to identify key trends and support data-driven decision-making, demonstrating strong analytical, problem-solving, and business interpretation skills.

Insight Generation and Report ¶

Key Insights Summary¶

  • Brand Performance & Distribution: Zara leads in product volume (300+ items), while Gap has the lowest inventory footprint. Brands like Mango and Banana Republic maintain highly similar stock levels.
  • Seasonal Trends: Summer is the peak season for product variety, holding nearly 600 items, whereas Winter sees the lowest volume (500+ items).
  • Inventory & Sizing: Accessories (no-size category) dominate the stock with 22.6% of total units. Standard apparel sizes (S–XXL) are distributed evenly, suggesting a balanced sizing strategy for clothing lines.
  • Pricing & Markdowns: There is a near-perfect correlation (0.92) between original and current prices. Most products are priced between 30 and 90, with the highest concentration at the 60.
  • Customer Feedback: The most common rating is 3.0, with many ratings also at 1.0 and 4.5, suggesting mixed customer satisfaction and possible differences in product quality.
  • Return Patterns: Bottoms and Outerwear experience the highest return rates (~16%), while Tops are the most kept items (12.8% return rate).

Overall Findings¶

The boutique follows a mid-range pricing strategy, with most products priced around 60 and a balanced mix of items across seasons. However, high return rates in categories like Bottoms and Outerwear, along with an average customer rating of 3.0, suggest issues with product fit or quality. Although the store maintains good inventory levels, improvements are needed in consistency. The large number of accessories in stock shows they are likely low-risk, fast-selling items that help maintain steady sales.

Recommendations¶

  • Reduce Returns: Check why Bottoms and Outerwear have high return rates. Find out if the problem is wrong sizing or poor quality, and fix it.
  • Improve Pricing Strategy: Outerwear brings high revenue but also has many returns. Focus on improving quality and slightly increase prices for best-selling items to maintain profit.
  • Clear Slow-Moving Stock: Use discounts and combo offers to sell low-performing items like Gap or Winter products (for example, offer a Winter accessory at 50% off when buying a Summer top).
  • Improve Customer Ratings: The average rating is 3.0. Start a loyalty program that rewards customers for writing helpful reviews to improve trust and future sales.
